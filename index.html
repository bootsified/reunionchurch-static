Our Story

Pastor Richard Ellis launched Reunion Church on October 5, 1997 in a small office in the Uptown area of Dallas, Texas. A group of 15 believers, devoted to the Lord, shared a common mission to reach the ALL of Dallas with the truth of the Gospel of Jesus Christ. Embracing the multi-cultural and multi-ethnic diversity of the city, the church sought to be a place where anyone could come to hear about Jesus, plant roots in His truth, and learn how to live lives that bring glory to God.

We began in an office in uptown Dallas, but the growth of the Reunion family has moved our Sunday morning worship to an elementary school, a high school auditorium, and then in September 2001 to our current location at the Dallas Convention Center Theater, also known as The Black Academy of Arts and Letters.

A Church. A Cross. A World

Our vision is not limited to our local church gathering or to Dallas. Christ died for all, not just those in our city. The vision is to reach all nations, all people, with the message of the life, death, burial and resurrection of the Lord Jesus Christ. Our efforts to help accomplish this goal are evident in our prayer and financial support of missionaries, our support of Christian media, and through ongoing teaching to help individuals understand the necessity of making an impact for the glory of God by boldly sharing His truth with the world.

Disciples Making Disciples

(Matthew 28:19-20)
Our ultimate purpose is to encourage individuals to establish a relationship with God through Jesus Christ and then help them grow as true followers of Christ (disciples). They in turn can then introduce another person to Jesus Christ and help them grow to maturity in their faith.

Richard Ellis - Pastor

In 1997, Richard & his family, along with 11 others, planted Reunion Church - a multi-cultural, multi-ethnic, non-denominational church, in the heart of Dallas. More »

Prior to that, he served for three years as Minister to Single Adults at the First Baptist Church of Dallas, Texas. For ten years Richard was the featured speaker for Lunch Talk, a weekly business luncheon, at the First Baptist Church of Dallas. He can be heard in Dallas on 100.7 FM weekdays at 6:30am and Sundays at 10:30am. Richard can also be heard on 444 radio stations in 45 states as well as richardellistalks.com. Richard is also one of the first stories to be featured on iamsecond.com.

Richard graduated from Baylor University in 1982, with a BA in Oral Communications and Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary in 1985 with an MDIV. He also did some of his Masters work at Dallas Theological Seminary. After graduating from seminary, he went into business for 10 years. Richard lived in England as a child while his father, Perry Ellis, was studying at Oxford University. His family then moved to Brazil where his parents were missionaries for many years. He is the sixth generation of pastors in his family.

Richard lives in Dallas with his wife, Rebecca. They have three daughters – Elizabeth, Victoria and Catherine.

Patrick Thomas - Associate Pastor

Patrick has been with Reunion since Sept 2001, where he currently serves as Associate Pastor (Music, Worship, and Marriage Ministries) and Elder. More »

Patrick has been involved in church music for more than 30 years. He is a skilled musician, singer, songwriter, & producer. He also has a passion for sharing the Word of God and began his public preaching ministry in December 1997.

After receiving the call to preach, Patrick felt called to relocate to Dallas in order to attend Dallas Theological Seminary (DTS), where he earned a ThM in Pastoral Ministries & Bible Exposition in 2004. While continuing to serve at Reunion, Patrick returned to DTS in 2015 - this time, accepting the position of Director of Chapel Music & Worship.

Patrick is married to Tiffany, and they have been blessed with four children—two boys and two girls: Nehemiah Andrew, Judah Elizabeth, Benjamin Daniel, and Esther Amariah.
